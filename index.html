NATIONAL SERVICE SCHEME

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a voluntary program for students that was launched by the Government of India in 1969 with the aim of promoting social consciousness and a sense of responsibility, discipline, and dignity of labor among youth. The program is implemented through educational institutions and provides opportunities for students to participate in various community service activities. At UIET, the NSS unit was established in 2005 and has since been actively involved in organizing various activities and events aimed at community service and social welfare. Some of the activities organized by the NSS unit at UIET include blood donation camps, health awareness programs, tree plantation drives, cleanliness drives, and educational campaigns in nearby villages. The NSS unit at UIET encourages students to actively participate in these activities and promotes a sense of social responsibility and leadership among them. NSS activities not only benefit the community but also help in the overall personality development of students and prepare them for leadership roles in the future.

WATER CONSERVATION

save water save life

Water conservation refers to the practice of using water wisely and efficiently to minimize water waste and ensure its sustainable use for current and future generations. It involves adopting habits, technologies, and strategies that reduce water consumption and preserve this valuable resource. Water conservation is crucial because fresh water is a finite resource, and many regions around the world face water scarcity or stress due to population growth, climate change, and unsustainable water management practices

BLOOD DONATION CAMP

GIVE BLOOD GIVE LIFE

Blood Donation Camp A blood donation camp is an event organized to encourage people to voluntarily donate blood. Blood donation camps are usually set up by non-profit organizations, hospitals, or government agencies in order to increase the availability of blood for those in need. During a blood donation camp, trained medical professionals collect blood from willing donors. The donated blood is then tested to ensure that it is safe for transfusion, and it is typically stored in a blood bank until it is needed. .

Ek Kadam Sawachta Ki Aur

Swachhta Abhiyan Swachhta Abhiyan, also known as the Clean India Mission, is a nationwide cleanliness campaign launched by the Indian government on October 2, 2014. The campaign aims to make India a clean and hygienic country by promoting cleanliness, hygiene, and sanitation practices among the citizens.
